### User pain points - Power Automate:

 

1. **Complexity**: While Power Automate is designed to be a low-code/no-code automation platform, some users may still find it challenging to create and manage workflows due to the complexity of the platform. The interface can be overwhelming, especially for non-technical users, and it may take time to understand the different types of workflows, triggers, and actions.
2. **Limited connectors:** While Power Automate provides a wide range of connectors to popular business applications and services, there may be some gaps in the available integrations. This can be a pain point for businesses that use less common tools or systems, as they may need to build custom connectors or use workarounds to integrate these systems with Power Automate.
3. **Cost**: While Power Automate provides a free version, more advanced features such as AI Builder, custom connectors, and premium connectors require a paid license. This can be a pain point for businesses that need access to these features but may not have the budget to pay for them.
4. **Performance**: Power Automate workflows may experience slow execution times or fail to run properly, which can be a pain point for businesses that rely on automation to streamline their processes. Additionally, if the workflow relies on external services or applications, any issues with those services can also impact the performance of the workflow.
5. **Debugging workflows:** When workflows fail to execute or produce unexpected results, it can be challenging to identify the root cause and fix the issue. Debugging workflows may require reviewing error logs, testing individual workflow steps, or consulting with technical support, which can be time-consuming and frustrating.
6. **Scalability**: Power Automate may not be suitable for businesses with complex or high-volume workflows that require high levels of scalability. As the volume of workflows increases, businesses may experience performance issues or encounter limitations with the number of concurrent workflows that can run.
7. **Limited customization:** Power Automate provides a range of pre-built templates and actions, but users may find it challenging to customize these templates or create custom workflows that meet their specific needs. This can be a pain point for businesses that require highly tailored automation solutions.
8. **Compatibility issues:** Power Automate workflows may encounter compatibility issues with certain systems or applications, which can prevent workflows from running or produce unexpected results. This can be a concern for businesses that rely on a wide range of systems and tools.
